Volume I

The Weight
of War

Marcus Cole came home. His body did.

But what he saw in the dust of Fallujah — the faces, the fire, the silence of men who would never speak again — that stayed behind enemy lines in his mind, refusing to surrender.

"I put my boots on every morning like a man who knows what he's doing. Nobody had to know that inside, I was still walking that street."

This is his story. Not the Army's version. Not the official report. Told from where he stood — in the dark, at 3am, holding the weight no one else could see.
